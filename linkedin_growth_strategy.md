# Kaelux LinkedIn Growth & Rotation Strategy

To maximize free organic reach on a LinkedIn Company Page, we must exploit the algorithm's "newness" bias. Free job postings get a massive algorithmic boost in the first 72 hours, then flatline once the free applicant limit is reached. 

Here is your exact blueprint for rotating jobs and content to continuously drive new followers.

## 🔄 Job Rotation Strategy (The "14-Day Cycle")

Because engagement stops after 3-4 days on free posts, leaving a job up for 30 days is a waste of your single free slot. 

**The Rule**: Close the active job after 14 days, wait a few days to let the algorithm "breathe," and launch the next one.

### The Immediate Rotation Schedule
1. **Right Now**: Close the "Founding Marketing and Growth Specialist" job. It has done its job (got you 13 followers) and is now algorithmically dead.
2. **Tomorrow**: Post the **"Founding AI Engineer"** role using your free slot. This is your flagship role. It signals deep technical ambition and will attract high-quality engineering talent and CTOs.
3. **In 14 Days**: Close the Founding AI Engineer role. 
4. **In 17 Days**: Post the **"Systems Integration Engineer"** role in the free slot. 

*Note: You can always re-post the Marketing job 2 months from now as a "new" listing to get another 72-hour algorithmic boost.*

---

## 📈 Content Posting Strategy (2x a Week)

Jobs drive rapid follower spikes, but content builds the authority that turns followers into clients. Post on the **Kaelux Company Page** 2 times a week. 

### Week 1: The Relaunch
*   **Post 1 (Today/Tomorrow)**: Post the **"Day 2 Expertise & Vision"** text we wrote (`linkedin_day2_post.txt`). Remember to put the Hashnode link in the comments.
*   **Post 2 (3 days later)**: Announce the new Founding AI Engineer role on the feed. 
    *   *Caption idea:* "We are expanding the core engineering team at Kaelux. We're looking for builders who understand the difference between chat-wrappers and production-grade agentic systems. Apply below."

### Week 2: Technical Authority
*   **Post 3**: Share a specific insight from your ViperMesh or PromptTriage research. (e.g., "Why Naive RAG fails in production and how we solved it.") Point this to a Hashnode article.
*   **Post 4**: Share a visual. A screenshot of your terminal, a diagram of your architecture, or a snippet of your Markdown documentation. Developers love seeing the "kitchen," not just the final meal.

### Week 3: Systems & Solutions
*   **Post 5**: Highlight your "OpenClaw Cloud" hosting infrastructure. Talk about the difficulty of securing AI in the cloud.
*   **Post 6**: Announce the new "Systems Integration Engineer" role on the feed.

## 💡 Top 3 Rules for Company Pages
1. **Never use "I"**: Always use "We/Our" on the Kaelux page. Save "I" for when you share the post to your personal Kristofer Jussmann profile.
2. **First Comment Linking**: Never put URLs in the main post. LinkedIn punishes external links. Put them in the first comment.
3. **Engage as the Founder**: Within 10 minutes of the Kaelux page posting something, like and comment on it from your personal profile to jumpstart the algorithm.
